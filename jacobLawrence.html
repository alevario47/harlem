Jacob Lawrence

During the 1930s and 1940s Harlem Renaissance, African American painter, and printmaker Jacob Lawrence achieved fame. He was raised in Harlem in New York City despite being born in Atlantic City, New Jersey. Lawrence was noted for his distinctive blend of realism and abstraction, which frequently featured strong geometric patterns and vivid, brilliant colors. The "Migration Series," a collection of 60 panels by Lawrence that documents the mass movement of African Americans from the rural south to the urban north, is one of his most well-known pieces. The Migration Series, is regarded as one of the most important pieces of African American art. Another notable works by Lawrence, besides the Migration Series, is "The Builders", which shows construction workers working on a building while black families gather and unwind in the foreground. More important than his individual artistic accomplishments were Lawrence's contribution to the art world. In addition to the University of Washington, Pratt Institute, and Black Mountain College, he taught at several other universities. Although he went died in 2000, his contributions to the world of art have had a lasting influence on creators, art historians, and art enthusiasts.
